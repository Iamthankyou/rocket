## Key Facts & Numbers

### Artemis Program (Return to Moon)
| Phase | Timeline | Objective |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| Artemis I | Nov 16-Dec 10, 2022 | Completed. Uncrewed SLS/Orion test, 25.5-day flight |
| Artemis II | 2025-2026 | Crewed lunar orbit, 4 astronauts, 10-day mission |
| Artemis III | 2027-2028 | First woman on Moon, 2-4 astronauts, 1 week surface |
| Artemis IV+ | 2030+ | Sustained presence, Lunar Gateway station |

**SLS Specs:**
- Height: 98m (taller than Saturn V by 1m)
- Thrust: 8.8 million lbs at launch
- Capacity: 70 metric tons to LEO, 27 tons to Moon
- Cost: ~$2B per launch | Development: $50B+ (2011-2022)
- Engines: 4x RS-25 core + 2x SRBs (6M lbf each)

**Lunar Gateway:**
- Orbit: 300-74,000 km elliptical lunar orbit
- Crew: 4 astronauts | First module: 2026-2027
- Purpose: Moon orbit staging point, fuel depot, habitat

### SpaceX Starship
- Height: 120m (Super Heavy + Starship)
- Diameter: 9m | Dry mass: 120 metric tons
- Fuel: 1,200 metric tons methane + LOX
- Thrust: Super Heavy 74 MN (33 Raptors) + Starship 15 MN (9 Raptors)
- Payload: 100-150 metric tons to LEO, 50 tons to Mars
- Construction: Stainless steel (easy repair/welding)
- Booster catch: "Chopstick" arms on launch tower
- Designed for 24-hour reflight capability

**Mars Timeline (aspirational):**
- 2026-2027: Uncrewed cargo missions test landing/refueling
- 2028-2029: First crewed Mars departure possible
- 2029-2035: Initial Mars base, up to 1000 people per transfer window
- Long-term: Self-sustaining Mars colony, multi-planet species

**Mars Mission Profile:**
- Hohmann transfer: ~9 months outbound, ~6 months return
- Total mission: ~2.5 years (1 year surface minimum)
- Refueling: ISRU — produces methane from CO2 atmosphere (Sabatier reaction)

### Advanced Propulsion Technologies

| Technology | Isp | Mars Transit | Status |
|-----------|-----|-------------|--------|
| Chemical (RP-1/LOX) | 360s | 9 months | Mature |
| Chemical (H2/LOX) | 470s | 9 months | Mature |

**NERVA Program (1970-73):** Tested nuclear thermal at 825s Isp, cancelled for budget
**Current NTP:** NASA NTREES + DARPA programs targeting 2030s

### Long-term Vision (2030-2100+)
**Mars Colony Phases:**
- Phase 1 (2030-2050): 10-100 person settlement, nuclear power, ISRU
- Phase 2 (2050-2100): 1,000+ residents, hydroponic food, 3D printing habitats
- Phase 3 (2100+): Terraforming concepts, 100,000+ year timeline

**Space Elevator:** Tether to 35,786 km, carbon nanotubes (>130 GPa), estimated $10-20B, possibly 2100+

**Interstellar:** Generation ships to Proxima Centauri (4.24 ly), 50-500 year transit, late 22nd century earliest

## Key Concepts
- ISRU (Tan dung tai nguyen tai cho): producing fuel on Mars from atmosphere
- Dong co nhiet hat nhan (Nuclear thermal propulsion): 2-3x better than chemical
- Dong co ion (Ion drive): very high Isp, very low thrust, long burns
- Thang may vu tru (Space elevator): tether concept, requires carbon nanotubes
- Tau the he (Generation ship): multi-generational interstellar travel

## Content Depth
- Artemis: Political significance — first woman, first person of color on Moon
- SLS controversy: $50B+ development, $2B/launch vs SpaceX's reusable approach
- Starship: Stainless steel choice (counter-intuitive, heavier but cheaper/easier to repair)
- ISRU: Game-changer — don't carry return fuel, produce it on Mars (Sabatier reaction)
- Nuclear thermal: Already tested in 1970s (NERVA), 825s Isp proven, just not operationalized
- Ion drives: Already used on 100+ satellites, proven technology, just needs scaling
- Space tourism: Democratization of space — from government astronauts to paying customers
- Vietnamese angle: VinSpace satellite program, growing STEM interest, future participation
