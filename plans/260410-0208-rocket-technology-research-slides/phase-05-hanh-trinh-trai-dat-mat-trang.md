### Apollo 11 Timeline (July 16-24, 1969)
- **Jul 16, 09:32 UTC** — Launch from Kennedy Space Center, LC-39A
- **S-IC (1st stage):** 2 min 41s, reaches 68 km
- **S-II (2nd stage):** 6 min 26s, reaches LEO ~185 km
- **S-IVB (3rd stage):** First burn = parking orbit; second burn = TLI
- **Jul 16, 14:22 UTC** — TLI burn: 5 min 48s, Dv 3,258 m/s
- **3-day coast:** 384,400 km transit, course corrections
- **Jul 19, 17:22 UTC** — LOI burn: 4 min 40s, Dv 1,068 m/s, orbit at 111 km
- **Jul 20, 20:17:40 UTC** — "The Eagle has landed" (Mare Tranquillitatis)
- **Jul 21, 02:56 UTC** — Armstrong's first step: "One small step for man..."
- **Surface time:** 21h 31min total, EVA 2h 31min (Armstrong), 1h 10min (Aldrin)
- **Samples collected:** 21.5 kg moon rock
- **Jul 21, 17:54 UTC** — Ascent from lunar surface (7 min 40s burn)
- **Jul 22, 06:13 UTC** — Trans-Earth Injection burn: 3 min 47s, Dv 1,006 m/s
- **Jul 24, 16:50 UTC** — Splashdown in Pacific, recovered by USS Hornet
- **Total mission:** 8 days, 3 hours, 18 minutes

### Crew
| Astronaut | Role | Key Fact |
|-----------|------|----------|
| Neil Armstrong | Commander | First human on Moon, 2h 31min moonwalk |
| Buzz Aldrin | LM Pilot | Second on Moon, 1h 10min moonwalk, qualified geologist |
| Michael Collins | CM Pilot | Orbited Moon 30 times solo, never walked on surface |

### Orbital Mechanics
- **Hohmann Transfer:** Semi-major axis ~192,200 km, apogee at Moon
- **LOI approach velocity:** ~1.0 km/s relative to Moon
- **Lunar orbit:** 300 km x 111 km, period ~2 hours
- **Powered descent:** 12 min from 9 km altitude, braking at 75m hover, landing at 0.5-1.5 m/s
- **Ascent burn:** 7 min, Dv ~2,400 m/s to lunar orbit

### Communication Architecture
- Ground stations: Goldstone (CA), Honeysuckle Creek (Australia), Madrid (Spain)
- Bandwidth: 1.024 kbps uplink, 2.4 kbps downlink
- Antennas: 64m Goldstone dish primary
- Signal strength: millionths of a watt received

### Saturn V for Apollo
- Height: 111m | Mass: 2,970,000 kg | 3 stages
- S-IC: 5x F-1 engines, 7,600 kN, LOX/RP-1, 168s burn
- S-II: 5x J-2 engines, 5,100 kN, LOX/LH2, 360s burn
- S-IVB: 1x J-2 engine, 1,020 kN, LOX/LH2, TLI burn
- Apollo stack: CM 5,900 kg + SM 24,500 kg + LM 16,600 kg = 130,000 kg payload

## Key Concepts
- Quy dao chuyen Hohmann (Hohmann transfer): most fuel-efficient path
- Phun Tang Xuyen Mat Trang (Trans-Lunar Injection): the critical burn leaving Earth orbit
- Chen quy dao Mat Trang (Lunar Orbit Insertion): braking into Moon's gravity
- Ha canh dong luc (Powered descent): 12-minute controlled descent
- Rendezvous quy dao (Orbital rendezvous): LM returns to dock with CM

## Content Depth
- Open with scale: 384,400 km = 30 Earth diameters placed side by side
- TLI is the commitment point: after this burn, you're going to the Moon
- LOI is equally critical: miss it and you fly past Moon into deep space
- Landing drama: Armstrong manually piloted last 30 seconds to avoid boulder field, 25s fuel remaining
- Collins' perspective: alone behind Moon (radio blackout), most isolated human ever
- Return: TEI burn must be precise — slight error means missing Earth's atmosphere
- Legacy: 382 kg total samples across 6 Apollo landings, retroreflectors still used today for laser ranging
