### ISS Specifications
- Altitude: 408 km | Velocity: 7.66 km/s (27,600 km/h)
- Orbital period: 92.68 min (15.5 orbits/day)
- Crew: 6-7 | Modules: 15 (Russia 5, USA 8, ESA 1, JAXA 1)
- Size: 109m x 73m | Mass: 420,000 kg
- Solar arrays: 2,500 m2, 120 kW power
- Operational since 2000 (25+ years continuous human presence)
- 270+ astronauts served

### Life in Microgravity
- Bone density loss: 1% per month
- Muscle atrophy: 10% per week without exercise
- Fluid shift: 1.5-2L toward head in first days
- Radiation: 150-200 mrem/year (vs 300 mrem/year on Earth)
- Career dose limit: ~1 Sv cumulative
- Water recycling: 93% recovery rate
- Atmosphere: 21% O2, 78% N2, 101.3 kPa (same as sea level)

### Lagrange Points
- L1, L2: 1.5 million km from Earth, unstable (need stationkeeping)
- L2: JWST location (space telescopes, cosmic background)
- L4, L5: Naturally stable (Trojan asteroids cluster here)
- Physics: gravitational + orbital motion equilibrium points

### Famous Missions Timeline

**Early Era (1957-1965):**
- Sputnik 1 (Oct 4, 1957): First satellite, 83.6 kg, 96 min orbit
- Vostok 1 (Apr 12, 1961): Gagarin, 108 min, 315 km max altitude
- Mercury-Atlas 6 (Feb 20, 1962): John Glenn, first American orbiting

**Lunar Era (1966-1972):**
- Apollo 8 (Dec 1968): First crewed lunar orbit
- Apollo 11 (Jul 1969): First Moon landing
- Apollo 13 (Apr 1970): "We've had a problem" — safe return via LM
- Total: 12 astronauts walked on Moon, 382 kg samples

**Deep Space (1970s-1980s):**
- Voyager 1 (Sep 5, 1977): Now 24.8 billion km away, still transmitting (47+ years)
- Voyager 2 (Aug 20, 1977): Only spacecraft to visit all 4 gas giants
- Golden Record: sounds/images of Earth, 1.5 billion-year lifespan
- Speed: 17 km/s (Voyager 1), heading toward interstellar space

**Modern Mars Exploration:**
- Spirit (2003-2010): Discovered water evidence, 6 years
- Opportunity (2003-2018): 14.9 years, dust storm ended mission
- Curiosity (2011-present): Gale Crater, methane detection
- Perseverance (2020-present): Ingenuity helicopter (70+ flights, designed for 5)

**Asteroid Missions:**
- OSIRIS-REx: 248g from Bennu, returned Sep 24, 2023 (largest since Apollo)
- Hayabusa2 (Japan): Ryugu samples, Feb 2021 return

## Key Concepts
- Quy dao dia tinh (Geostationary orbit): satellite appears stationary above equator
- Vi trong luc (Microgravity): free-fall environment, 10^-6 g
- Diem Lagrange (Lagrange points): gravitational equilibrium positions
- Ho tro su song (Life support): O2 generation, CO2 scrubbing, water recycling
- Khong gian lien sao (Interstellar space): beyond heliosphere (Voyager 1 exited 2012)

## Content Depth
- Orbits: Explain why GEO is exactly 35,786 km (orbital period = Earth rotation)
- ISS: Largest human-made structure in space, visible with naked eye, cost $150B+
- Microgravity: Not "zero gravity" — ISS has 90% of Earth's gravity, but freefall cancels it
- Gagarin: 108 minutes that changed humanity; ejected from capsule and parachuted separately
- Voyager: Designed for 5-year mission, operating 47+ years; power from plutonium-238 RTG
- Apollo 13: Oxygen tank explosion, crew used LM as lifeboat, greatest rescue in space history
- Perseverance/Ingenuity: Helicopter flew in atmosphere 1% of Earth's density — engineering triumph
- OSIRIS-REx: Touch-and-go sample collection, 248g = largest extraterrestrial sample since Apollo
